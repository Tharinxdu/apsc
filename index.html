Message from the President of NSASL
Dr. Gamini Pathirana

President, National Stroke Association of Sri Lanka (NSASL)
Chair, Asia Pacific Stroke Conference 2026

It is a great privilege and honour to host the Asia Pacific Stroke Conference (APSC) 2026, scheduled to take place from 26th to 28th November 2026 at Block 2 of the Bandaranaike Memorial International Conference Hall (BMICH) in the vibrant city of Colombo, Sri Lanka.

The conference theme, “Enhancing Stroke Management: Overcoming Challenges and Fostering Regional Synergies,” reflects our shared commitment to advancing stroke care across the Asia–Pacific region. APSC 2026 will bring together leading international and regional experts to present the latest scientific developments and to explore innovative, context-specific strategies addressing the diverse needs of our communities.

Often celebrated as the “Pearl of the Indian Ocean,” Sri Lanka provides an inspiring setting for this important gathering. The island seamlessly blends modern infrastructure with centuries of rich culture and heritage. From Colombo, delegates can easily explore iconic destinations such as the ancient cities of Sigiriya and Anuradhapura, the mist-covered tea plantations of Nuwara Eliya, or the country’s renowned golden beaches. November is one of the most pleasant times to visit Sri Lanka, offering a tropical yet comfortable climate ideal for both professional engagement and leisure.

APSC 2026 is the annual regional meeting of the Asia Pacific Stroke Organisation (APSO) and will be hosted in Colombo by the National Stroke Association of Sri Lanka (NSASL). We are deeply grateful to APSO for entrusting Sri Lanka with this prestigious meeting, and for its continued guidance, support, and commitment in organising this landmark event.

This conference will be conducted as a fully in-person meeting, designed to promote meaningful face-to-face interactions and networking. The scientific programme will feature plenary lectures, focused symposia, and interactive workshops, offering participants valuable opportunities to learn from world-class faculty and to exchange ideas with colleagues from across the region.

Colombo is easily accessible via Bandaranaike International Airport, with convenient transport links to the city and the conference venue. A wide range of hotels, dining options, and cultural attractions will ensure a comfortable and memorable stay for all delegates.

Our organising team is committed to delivering APSC 2026 as both a landmark scientific event and a warm introduction to Sri Lankan hospitality. We look forward to welcoming you to Colombo for three days of learning, collaboration, and friendship.

Discover Sri Lanka—where ancient heritage meets modern medicine.

Message from the President of APSO
Prof. Nijasri C. Suwanwela, M.D

President, Asia Pacific Stroke Organisation (APSO)

On behalf of the Asia Pacific Stroke Organisation (APSO), it is my great pleasure to invite you to the Asia Pacific Stroke Conference (APSC) 2026, to be held in Colombo, Sri Lanka, from 26–28 November 2026.

The Asia–Pacific region, home to more than half of the world’s population, bears the greatest burden of stroke worldwide. Yet, it is also a region of tremendous innovation, diversity, and resilience. APSC continues to serve as the leading scientific platform where clinicians, researchers, nurses, allied health professionals, and health policymakers come together to share discoveries, exchange experiences, and forge partnerships that advance stroke care and prevention across borders.

As we gather in Colombo, the Scientific Committee has prepared an excellent and inspiring program that not only showcases the latest scientific advances but also offers practical, regionally relevant insights through interactive sessions and opportunities to strengthen collaboration across the Asia–Pacific. Together, we can shape a future where stroke care is more equitable, effective, and accessible for all.

I warmly welcome you to join us in Colombo and be part of this meaningful journey for stroke in the Asia–Pacific region.
